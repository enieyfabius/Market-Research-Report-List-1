<p><p>The naphthalene market is primarily divided into two types: coal tar-based and petroleum-based naphthalene. Coal tar naphthalene is derived from the destructive distillation of coal, offering high purity and is often used in dyes, pharmaceuticals, and insecticides. Petroleum naphthalene, obtained from crude oil refining, is more cost-effective and predominantly utilized in the production of fragrances, plastics, and as a solvent. Both types serve diverse industrial applications, catering to varying quality and economic requirements in the market.</p></p>
