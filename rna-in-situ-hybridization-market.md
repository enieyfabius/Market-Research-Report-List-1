<p><p>RNA In Situ Hybridization (ISH) encompasses various techniques for detecting RNA within tissue samples. GISH (Genomic In Situ Hybridization) assesses whole genome hybridization, useful for identifying chromosomal abnormalities. FISH (Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization) offers specific RNA sequence detection using fluorescent probes, often for genetic research. mFISH (multicolor FISH) extends FISH by allowing the simultaneous observation of multiple RNA targets, enhancing diagnostic precision. PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction) supplements these methods, amplifying RNA fragments for detailed analysis and broader application in research and diagnostics.</p></p>
