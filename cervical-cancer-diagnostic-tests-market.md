<p><p>The cervical cancer diagnostic tests market encompasses various methodologies for detecting the disease. Pap smear tests involve collecting cells from the cervix to identify abnormalities. Colposcopy offers a magnified view for further examination after abnormal Pap results. HPV testing detects high-risk human papillomavirus strains associated with cervical cancer. Endocervical curettage (ECC) collects tissue from the cervical canal, while cone biopsy involves removing a cone-shaped section of cervical tissue for detailed analysis. Other methods may include imaging and biomarker tests, broadening diagnostic options.</p></p>
