<p><p>The large utilities and transmission market is categorized into three voltage levels: low voltage, medium voltage, and high voltage. Low voltage systems typically operate below 1 kV, serving residential and small commercial needs. Medium voltage, ranging from 1 kV to 35 kV, is used for regional distribution networks. High voltage systems, exceeding 35 kV, are essential for long-distance transmission of electricity, facilitating the transfer of power from generation sites to substations, thereby ensuring efficient grid operations.</p></p>
