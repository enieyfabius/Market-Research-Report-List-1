<p><p>The quadrivalent flu vaccine market is segmented into three main types: intramuscular injection, nasal spray, and intradermal shot. Intramuscular injections are the most common method, delivering the vaccine deep into the muscle, ensuring robust immunity. Nasal sprays offer a needle-free alternative, suitable for healthy individuals, especially children. Intradermal shots involve injecting the vaccine into the skin, providing a smaller dosage with a potentially milder immune response. Each method caters to varying patient preferences and specific medical needs.</p></p>
